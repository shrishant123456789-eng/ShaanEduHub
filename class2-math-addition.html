3.1 The Plus Sign (+)

The symbol for addition looks like a cross: +

It is called PLUS.

The answer we get after adding is called the SUM or TOTAL.

3.2 Adding Big Numbers (Up to 999)

We know 1-digit numbers (like 5, 8) and 2-digit numbers (like 24, 99). Now let's add 3-digit numbers!

  • O = Ones
  • T = Tens
  • H = Hundreds

Always remember: Start from the Right (Ones place)! üëâ

3.3 Addition Without Carry (Simple)

This is when the sum of digits is less than 9. No need to move numbers!

Example: 123 + 215

Step 1 (Ones): 3 + 5 = 8
Step 2 (Tens): 2 + 1 = 3
Step 3 (Hundreds): 1 + 2 = 3
Answer: 338

3.4 Addition With Carry (Grouping) üîÑ

Sometimes, when we add, the number gets too big for one room! If the sum is 10 or more, we must CARRY the 1 to the next neighbor.

Example: 28 + 14

Step 1 (Ones): 8 + 4 = 12.
Oh no! 12 has two digits. Keep 2 in Ones place. Carry 1 to Tens place.

Step 2 (Tens): 2 + 1 + (Carry 1) = 4.
Answer: 42
